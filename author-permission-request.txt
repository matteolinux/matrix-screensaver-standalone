Subject: Permission to publish a standalone wrapper for Matrix Screensaver

Hi Monroe,

First of all, thank you for your Matrix screensaver. I really like it and wanted to keep using it on recent macOS versions.

Because of the known `legacyScreenSaver` issue you mention in your release notes, I experimented with an alternative approach: instead of installing `Matrix.saver` through System Settings, I built a small standalone macOS wrapper app that loads your `Matrix.saver` directly and displays it fullscreen.

The wrapper does not modify your screensaver engine. It uses your original `Matrix.saver` bundle as the rendering component, but runs it from a normal Cocoa app process instead of Apple's `legacyScreenSaver` process.

What I added:

- a standalone `Matrix Screensaver.app`;
- a menu bar item to start the screensaver;
- a small settings window for color, glyph size, and 3D fade;
- packaging as a `.dmg` with drag-to-Applications installation;
- build/package scripts and documentation;
- attribution to you and a link to your original project.

The reason for this approach is to avoid the macOS `legacyScreenSaver` path, while still preserving your original Matrix screensaver behavior.

Before publishing anything, I wanted to ask for your permission.

Would you be comfortable with me publishing:

1. the source code for the standalone wrapper; and
2. a downloadable `.dmg` that includes your original `Matrix.saver` embedded inside the app?

I would clearly credit you as the author of the original Matrix screensaver and link back to your GitHub repository and releases.

If you prefer, I can also publish only the wrapper source and require users to download `Matrix.saver` separately from your official releases.

Thanks again for your work.

Best,
Matteo
